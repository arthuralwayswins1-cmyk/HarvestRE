§ 03 · The Problem We Solve

§ When to Call GERE

GERE is most valuable before the decision hardens.

The right time to engage is while the structure, price, or path can still be improved.

I.

Before selling a property

When an owner is thinking about selling, the real work starts before a broker is engaged. GERE identifies title issues, loan timing constraints, lease documentation gaps, physical condition items, financial reporting weaknesses, and other issues that affect pricing and buyer leverage.

II.

Before signing a letter of intent

A letter of intent can lock a client into the wrong assumptions before the real estate has been structured correctly. GERE is often engaged when a proposed sale, purchase, leaseback, or business transaction has real estate consequences that need to be understood before any commitment is made.

III.

Before buying a building for the business

Owner-occupied real estate decisions require more than market access. GERE helps clients evaluate own versus lease tradeoffs, pricing, financing structures, property fit, condition issues, and the business implications of ownership before capital is committed.

IV.

When business & real estate need to be separated

Many private companies own the building they occupy but have never structured the real estate independently. GERE helps clients think through leaseback structures, bundled sale risks, value preservation, and the proper treatment of the real estate when the business is being sold or recapitalized.

V.

When inherited real estate becomes a real decision

Families often inherit operating real estate without a clear picture of market value, capital needs, documentation quality, or sale readiness. GERE helps create an objective framework for deciding whether to hold, improve, refinance, or sell.

VI.

Before accepting a lease or renewal by default

Tenants and users often assume renewal is the easiest answer. Sometimes it is. Sometimes it is not. GERE helps evaluate renewal, relocation, assignment, and occupancy alternatives with a full view of economics, flexibility, and business impact.

The right time to call is before the path feels fixed.

№ 001 Pre-Transaction Readiness Confidential · Private Family

Family-Owned Multifamily Asset

A family ownership group needed a complete view of whether to sell or continue holding a 24-unit apartment property. GERE identified an unrecorded deed transfer, loan timing risk against an 18-month maturity window, six units renting below market, documentation gaps that would have stalled buyer underwriting, and deferred maintenance items across three buildings. Three strategic alternatives were evaluated on a common financial basis, from an as-is sale to a full value-add hold program.

The family received a clear framework for sale readiness, hold economics, and per-member net proceeds under each scenario before the transaction process began.

№ 002 Business Sale Advisory Confidential · PE Transaction

Business Sale with Embedded Real Estate

A second-generation fire protection contractor received private equity interest for the operating business, but the real estate had not been structured separately. GERE demonstrated that a bundled sale would produce negative net real estate proceeds after accounting for CMBS debt, a yield maintenance penalty exceeding $330,000, and the resulting tax liability. The building's independent market value was approximately 86% to 92% of the operating business enterprise value, yet was receiving no equivalent planning.

GERE recommended a coordinated leaseback structure that preserved approximately $2.5 million in real estate value. The owner moved from an economically broken path to a structure that protected both assets.

№ 003 Acquisition Advisory Confidential · Manufacturer

Owner-Occupied Industrial Acquisition

A manufacturing company considering the purchase of a 24,000 SF flex/industrial building needed to understand whether ownership made sense, how SBA 504 financing changed the equation, and which issues had to be resolved before contract. Harvest evaluated own versus lease economics over a 10-year horizon, reviewed comparable sales, identified a material roof condition requiring $160,000 to $200,000 in replacement cost, and confirmed that the asking price exceeded the comparable sales range.

The client received a clear conditional recommendation with three pre-contract conditions, a target negotiating range, and a structured acquisition path.

№ 004 Investor Due Diligence Confidential · Family Office

Multifamily Sponsor Opportunity

A family office considering a $6.8 million equity investment in an 84-unit multifamily sponsor deal engaged GERE for an independent review. Analysis revealed that the sponsor's renovation budget was 26% below current contractor pricing, and the LP operating agreement contained an uncapped capital call provision creating open-ended investor exposure. Harvest stress-tested the underwriting across occupancy, rent growth, exit cap rate, and renovation cost scenarios.

The investment remained viable but only with specific pre-closing protections: a revised renovation budget, a hard cap on capital calls, and a key person provision. Two conditions were designated as non-negotiable.
